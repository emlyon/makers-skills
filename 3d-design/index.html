Going further

Definitions

3D Design has multiple applications such as product design, architectural modeling, special effects for cinema, creation and animation of video game characters, constraints verification, aerodynamic studies and many more. This introduction is oriented towards 3D printing and product design.

There is a wide variety of 3D modeling tools, each one is oriented to a specific domain. The main 3D modeling techniques are :

  • Polygonal Modeling: the model si created by placing points in 3D space and connecting then by line segments to form polygons.
  • NURBS Modeling: the surface geometry is generated by curves driven by mathematical equations.
  • Solid Modeling: the objects are created by combining solid bodies into a single 3D design, this is the type of modeling used in Tinkercad
  • Generative Modeling: the geometry is automaticly generated by a program according to specific parameters.

Tools

  • Fusion 360, a very complete 3D modeling software (but much longer to master than Tinkercad). If you feel like a 3D designer, just go for it: Your student status allows you to download it for free!
  • Vectary, accessible on-browser modeling tools that offers lots of tools to create beautiful 3D images.
  • Onshape, on-browser CAD tool that offers professionnal modeling features, and it's free for students.
  • Rhino 3D, a professionnal NURBS modeling tool mostly used for product design and architecture.
  • Blender, a powerful but complex 3D creation suite, widely used by animators and game designers. Blender is completely free and open-source.
