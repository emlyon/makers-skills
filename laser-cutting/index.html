Going further

Definitions

Laser cutting, like many other CNC technologies, uses vector files to calculate the coordinates for its tool with micron accuracy. This precision, unattainable by human hand, allows the manufacture of much more complex parts than with traditional tools and even more simply. The only limit is the thickness of the materials that can be processed (10mm on makers' labs' machines).

Laser cutting is part of the family of substractive manufacturing techniques : the material is removed from a solid block to produce a part. Plasma cutting, CNC milling, and water jet cutting are other substractive manufacturing techniques similar to laser cutting.

The power and traveling speed of the laser determines the depth of the cut : for example to cut light materials like paper the laser moves fast and the power is set to low, for harder and thicker materials like wood the laser moves slowly and the power is set to high.
